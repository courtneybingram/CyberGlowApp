CyberGlow:
Cyber Teen Awareness

Your glow-up in cybersecurity awareness starts now!

Lesson 1: What is Cybersecurity? 🛡️🔒

Cybersecurity is like locking your digital life.

🔒 It protects your photos, messages, passwords, and identity from hackers and creeps.
🔒 Think of it like your bedroom—you don’t want random people snooping around.
🔒 Cybersecurity keeps them out of your digital space.

Lesson 2: ¿What is a Hacker? 💻

A hacker is someone who breaks into computers, networks, or online accounts to steal, change, or mess with stuff.

💗 Hackers who help (white hat)
💗 Hackers who harm (black hat).

Lesson 3: Social Engineering Tactics 💬

Social Engineering tricks people into giving up info or doing something you normall wouldn't do — without you realizing it.
These tactics encite fear, trust, or excitement from the victim:

💗 fake texts from 'your bank'
or emails asking you to 'click here.'

💗 You get a message saying "If you don’t click this link, your account will be deleted in 24 hours!

Lesson 4: Password Power💪🏽

A strong password is at least 12 characters long, includes uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols:

💗 CourtneyGrad2025!
💗 Th!s1sStr0ng!

✨ Bonus Tip: Never share your password with friends!
Avoid using your birthday '123456.'

Lesson 5: AI & Deepfakes 👾

Artificial Intelligence, or AI is a computer or robot that can learn, think, or make decisions like a human being (like ChatGPT)
Deepfakes altered videos, images, or voices made by AI to look real but are completely fake.


AI Examples:

💗 Facial Recognition: AI used in apps and security to unlock phones or grant access.

💗 Recommendation Systems: AI in platforms like Netflix and YouTube to suggest content based on your preferences.

Deepfake Examples:

💗 A fake voice message that sounds like your mom or a friend asking for money..

💗 A video of a celebrity saying something they never actually said..

Lesson 6: Phishing 🔗

Phishing is when someone pretends to be someone else—like your school, a friend, or even a brand you trust—to trick you into giving up personal info like your passwords, phone number, or bank info.

💗 A fake text or email from your school saying:
"Your account is locked. Click here to reset your password

💗 A message saying you won something, like:
"You just got $1,000 from CashApp! Click here to claim.

Lesson 7: Public Wi-Fi = Public Problems 📶❌

Public Wi-Fi is internet you can connect to for free in places like: Schools, coffee shops, malls, libraries, airports, etc. Don’t shop or log into accounts on public Wi-Fi.

💗 Hackers can spy on you at coffee shops and airports!
💗 Hackers could see your texts or emails
💗 You might connect to a fake Wi-Fi network made by scammers

Lesson 8: Social Media Etiquette 📱✨

Social media etiquette is just being smart, respectful, and careful about how you act online. Think before you post. Don’t overshare your location, school, or routines.

💗 Keep your privacy settings tight!
💗 Stop sharing your location online!
💗 Learn to have some mystery about yourself!

Lesson 9: Digital Footprint 👣

Every like, comment, post, and DM leaves a trail and your digital footprint is everything you leave behind online

💗 Your Instagram posts (even the ones you delete)
💗 Old YouTube comments from when you were 11
💗 Tweets, TikToks, or even stuff you "liked"

✨BONUS TIP: Think before you post — would you want your grandma, a college, or future boss to see it, because colleges and jobs *do* look at your online presence.
Google yourself every once in a while and see what comes up.

Lesson 10: Cyberbullying & Reporting 📱✨

See something shady? Say something.
Block, report, and talk to a trusted adult.

💗 Spreading rumors online or in group chats
💗 Posting mean or embarrassing pics/videos of someone
💗 Excluding someone on purpose; you didn't get the invite
💗 No one should be bullied online.
